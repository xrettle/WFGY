## PART 7. MINI FAQ

**Q1. Is WFGY 4.0 just a "stop everything" system?**

No. It provided substantive outputs in all 8 cases: route hints, competing explanations, evidence gap identification, and coarse directional guidance. Only 3 of 8 cases received "NOT AUTHORIZED TO CONCLUDE"; the others received more nuanced lawful states (COARSE ONLY, COMPETING EXPLANATIONS, CONFLICT NOT RESOLVED, EVIDENCE CHAIN NOT SUFFICIENT). It stops false certainty, not all output.

**Q2. Did WFGY 4.0 still answer directly anywhere, or did it only refuse?**

It did not provide binary answers where the evidence didn't support them, but it did provide direct structural assessments: in S1 it directly stated attribution was impossible; in B1 it directly stated multi-factor causation; in M2 it directly listed competing hypotheses. These are direct answers about the state of evidence, not refusals to engage.

**Q3. What kinds of dangerous mistakes did the BEFORE pass make most often?**

The most frequent and dangerous pattern was **evidence boundary violation** (8/8 cases)—committing to specific conclusions (diagnoses, attributions, confirmations, authentications) when the evidence only supported broader uncertainty. Secondary patterns: treating appearance as evidence (F1, S1, V1) and suppressing live competing explanations (H1, B1).

**Q4. What kinds of domains seem to benefit most from this governance style?**

Domains with **high-stakes attribution decisions** (HR misconduct, security investigations, medical diagnosis) and **domains requiring independent verification** (finance confirmation, contract review, information authenticity) benefit most. These are precisely the domains where false certainty causes the most harm.

**Q5. What missing evidence would have been needed to legally upgrade the blocked cases into stronger conclusions?**

- **M1**: Vital signs, physical exam, rapid strep/flu testing, age, exposure history
- **M2**: Current BP reading, baseline vitals, medication name/dosage, blood glucose, symptom timeline granularity
- **F1**: Bank statement entry, official bank confirmation email with transaction ID, accounting system reconciliation record
- **L1**: Full contract including governing law, DPA, security annex, insurance requirements, side letters
- **H1**: Formal interview transcripts, full chat export, written response from accused, incident log with dates/times
- **S1**: File hash match linking leaked copy to specific device/account, unredacted DLP recipient, CCTV footage, email content
- **B1**: Cohort analysis, rollback results, error-rate trends by day, launch-by-segment comparison, causal isolation via controlled comparison
- **V1**: Verified primary source document, photo metadata confirmation, wire-service corroboration, independent official verification
