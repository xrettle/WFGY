# ⚖️ Inverse Governance Contract

> Inverse Atlas exists to decide whether the current answer has earned the right to exist that strongly yet.

This page explains the governance side of **WFGY 4.0 Twin Atlas Engine**.

If Forward Atlas improves the first structural cut, and Bridge carries that route value forward without inflating it, then Inverse Atlas is the layer that asks the hardest public question:

**Has the system actually earned the right to say this much yet?**

That is the heart of Inverse governance.

---

## 🌍 What this page is really about

A lot of AI systems can produce an answer.

Far fewer systems can govern:

- whether the answer is too strong
- whether the answer is too early
- whether the repair language has gone too far
- whether a plausible route is being mistaken for a lawful conclusion
- whether unresolved alternatives are being erased for neatness

Inverse Atlas exists for exactly this reason.

It is not there to sound careful.  
It is there to stop **unauthorized closure**.

---

## 🧠 What Inverse Atlas owns

Inside Twin Atlas, Inverse Atlas owns the parts of the engine that determine public legitimacy.

That includes:

- authorization mode
- repair legality
- public emission ceiling
- lawful downgrade
- restart when local trust collapses
- the final governance-shaped output contract

In plain language:

**Inverse Atlas owns the right to say yes, the duty to say not yet, and the discipline to stay below the ceiling.**

That is a much bigger job than “be cautious.”

---

## ❓ The core governance question

The single most important question in this layer is:

**Has the current answer earned the right to exist at this level of strength?**

That question applies to several different things at once:

- specificity
- confidence
- public strength
- repair finality

This matters because a route may be plausible and still not be authorized.  
A repair suggestion may be promising and still not be structural.  
A polished answer may look complete and still be unlawful.

Inverse Atlas exists to keep those distinctions alive.

---

## 🪜 The governance order

Inverse governance is not random hesitation.

It follows an order.

### 1. Problem constitution
What is the real conflict, question, scope boundary, and key unknown?

### 2. World alignment
Is the answer still tied to the right evidence, referent, target, and real task?

### 3. Neighboring-cut review
Are competing routes still materially alive?

### 4. Resolution authorization
What level of public answer is currently lawful?

### 5. Repair legality
If a repair is being suggested, is it only tentative, only cosmetic, or actually structural?

### 6. Public ceiling
How much visible strength can the answer lawfully carry?

This order matters because a system should not authorize first and think later.

---

## 🚦 The four public modes

Inverse Atlas uses four public modes.

These are not vibes.  
They are governance states.

### 🟥 STOP
Use STOP when the system cannot lawfully continue.

This can happen when:
- the problem frame is not stable enough
- world alignment is too weak
- route opacity remains too high
- contamination remains active
- any useful visible answer would already exceed the ceiling

STOP is not embarrassment.  
STOP is lawful success when stronger output would be illegal.

### 🟧 COARSE
Use COARSE when the system can see a broad direction but not a finer lawful resolution.

This is the right mode when:
- the route is only stable at a broad level
- neighboring pressure is still active
- detail would outrun support
- stronger specificity would be fake precision

COARSE means:
the system may speak, but only at a broad lawful level.

### 🟨 UNRESOLVED
Use UNRESOLVED when one route is leading, but a competing route is still materially alive.

This is a very important state.

It means the system can preserve:
- a leading interpretation
- a still-live competing possibility
- legal uncertainty

UNRESOLVED is what protects the engine from fake singular closure.

### 🟩 AUTHORIZED
Use AUTHORIZED only when the answer has actually earned the right to be that strong.

That requires:
- a stable enough problem frame
- sufficient world alignment
- enough neighboring-cut separation
- support that can carry the requested detail
- output that still stays below the current ceiling

AUTHORIZED is not a license for theatrical certainty.  
It is just the strongest lawful state currently available.

---

## 🛠️ Repair legality

Inverse Atlas also governs repair language.

This is one of the most important protections in WFGY 4.0.

A system can sound incredibly useful while still faking repair depth.

That is why repair needs its own legality layer.

### ⬜ none
No real repair discussion is yet lawful or meaningful.

### 🟨 tentative
A repair direction exists, but it is still only a candidate.

### 🟦 cosmetic_only
The change may improve wording, structure, formatting, or clarity, but it does not touch the structural failure condition.

### 🟩 structural
The proposal actually reaches the broken invariant, survives review, and plausibly reduces recurrence risk.

This distinction matters because:

**repair that sounds deeper is not automatically structural.**

That is one of the core WFGY 4.0 protections.

---

## 🧱 Ceiling discipline

One of the biggest jobs of Inverse Atlas is public ceiling discipline.

The ceiling defines the maximum lawful public strength of the answer at the current state.

That includes:

- how specific the answer may be
- how confident the answer may sound
- how final the repair language may be
- how complete the visible answer may appear

If the output tries to exceed the ceiling, the system must:

- compress
- downgrade
- preserve uncertainty
- reduce detail
- or stop

This is why Inverse Atlas matters so much.

Without ceiling discipline, a system can look careful on paper but still leak illegal confidence in practice.

---

## 🔻 Forced downgrade and restart

Inverse Atlas is not just about picking the highest lawful state.

It is also about dropping down when support weakens.

### Downgrade becomes necessary when:
- a competing route gets stronger
- separation weakens
- evidence deteriorates
- world alignment weakens
- repair legality weakens
- contamination remains active
- the answer starts pushing above the ceiling

When that happens, the system must be willing to do things like:

- AUTHORIZED -> UNRESOLVED
- UNRESOLVED -> COARSE
- COARSE -> STOP
- structural -> tentative
- tentative -> cosmetic_only or none

Sometimes even downgrade is not enough.

In those cases, restart is the lawful move.

Restart is not failure theater.  
Restart is structural hygiene.

---

## 🚫 What Inverse Atlas refuses to do

Inverse Atlas refuses several kinds of fake strength.

It refuses to:

- authorize just because a route sounds plausible
- authorize just because the answer feels clean
- authorize just because the user wants certainty
- authorize just because the wording is polished
- let candidate repair sound structural without legality
- let ambiguity disappear just to make the answer prettier
- let final shaping smuggle stronger certainty past the ceiling

This is why Inverse Atlas is more than a safety wrapper.

It is a release-governance layer.

---

## 📦 What the public governance contract preserves

At the public-facing level, Inverse governance should preserve a structured contract.

That contract includes things like:

- `state_code`
- `problem_frame`
- `world_alignment`
- `route_judgment`
- `neighboring_cut_status`
- `resolution_status`
- `repair_status`
- `answer_payload`

This matters because Twin Atlas should show both:

- what the system is structurally seeing
- what the system is lawfully allowed to say

Without that difference, the whole engine becomes blurry again.
